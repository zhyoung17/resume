Data Journalism

I have done two internship stints at The Straits Times as a data journalist and newsdesk journalist.

Husband and Wives Income

Worked with a team of data visualisation developers and illustrators to produce my piece on more wives outearning their husbands.

My team and I analysed 3 sets of data comparing husband and wives' income. Following which, we conceptualised creative visualisation ideas, such as allowing readers to guess the spouse's income.

I am proud to share that this piece was one of the most read interactive news pieces in ST for 2023!

Analysing Shanti Pereira

I've also dabbled with sports analytics, and analysed data provided by the coaching team of Singapore national sprinter Shanti Pereira.

Working with a team of developers and designers, we created a groundbreaking piece analysing her technique using technical analysis.

The article was awarded The Straits Times cross media award of the quarter!

On top of managing projects, I was also involved in the curation of the weekly news quiz, which involves 7 questions concerning news that has happened over the week.

Print Journalism

On top of data analysis and digital journalism, I have also exhibited competency in writing and producing my own news articles. During my time at the Singapore newsdesk, I covered a wide range of social affairs in Singapore, including passport collection, the chicken shortage, multiple fire incidents.

My self-pitch, data-centric piece about the increasing maximum temperature in Singapore was chosen to be The Big Story on ST. It was chosen to be on page A3 and was selected as the editor's pick that day.
